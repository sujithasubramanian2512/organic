Tomato

Tomatoes are low-calorie and nutrient-dense, making them an important part of a balanced diet. Tomatoes offer various benefits and contribute to heart, cognitive (brain), and prostate health.

Onion

Raw onion is known to lower the production of LDL (bad cholesterol) and keep your heart healthy. The vitamin C (which remains intact while they are in the raw form) along with the phytochemicals present in onions helps build immunity.

Carrot

The antioxidants in carrots can reduce the presence of free radicals, thus reducing the overall risk of heart diseases. Long-term studies have indicated that consuming a higher intake of lutein and zeaxanthin reduces the risk of developing age-related degeneration of the eye.

Pomegranate

pomegranates are low in calories and fat but high in fiber, vitamins, and minerals. Pomegranates are rich in an array of antioxidants that help protect your cells from free radical damage.

Papaya

The papaya is a tropical fruit high in vitamins C and A, as well as fiber and healthy plant compounds. It also contains an enzyme called papain, used to tenderize meat.

Kiwi

Kiwis are nutritious fruits that are high in vitamin C, fiber, and plant compounds with antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Learn how kiwis may benefit your heart, digestion, and overall health, and get tips on how to eat them
